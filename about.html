About Tastes of Africa

Why We Made This Site

We’re not a big company or a fancy food magazine. We’re just a small group of friends who grew up in different corners of Africa, and we missed the taste of home. This site started as a way to swap recipes and stories with each other, and it’s grown into something we hope will make you hungry, curious, and maybe a little nostalgic too.

What African Food Means to Us

African food isn’t just about what’s on the plate. It’s about the laughter in the kitchen, the smell of spices that lingers on your hands, and the way a simple meal can bring a whole family (or neighborhood) together. We believe every dish has a story, and every cook has a memory worth sharing.

A Small Story

I still remember the first time I tried to make jollof rice on my own. I burned the bottom, used way too much pepper, and my little brother said it tasted like “spicy smoke.” But we ate every bite, laughing the whole time. That’s the real flavor of Africa—imperfect, bold, and full of heart.
